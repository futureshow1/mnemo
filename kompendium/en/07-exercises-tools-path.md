# 07. Exercises, tools, and a personal path

## A. A starter set of exercises

### Warm-ups (5 min/day)

**1. Room scan.** Enter a room you have not seen today. Look 30 seconds, leave, list everything you remember on paper. Return and check. Progress: from 30 s / 10 items to 20 s / 15.

**2. Reverse digits.** Say a sequence (3 7 2 9 1) and recite it backwards (1 9 2 7 3). Add a digit each day. Most adults reach 7–8 (Miller's working memory limit).

**3. Word chain.** Open a dictionary at random, pick 5 words, build an absurd sentence linking them in order. *"Under the lantern stood a crocodile in an apron eating cheesecake with a horseshoe dipped in ink."* One reading — five words.

### Memory palace (beginner)

**Build your first palace.** Choose a familiar space. Set 10 fixed points in order. Walk the path 5 times in your mind until automatic. **A one-time investment.** This palace will be yours for years.

**Shopping list in the palace.** Take 8–10 items, place each at the next locus with absurd images. In the store, walk the palace mentally. You remember everything without notes.

**Day plan in the palace.** Each morning, place 6–8 tasks in the palace. In the evening return — what happened?

### Number-shape peg system

**Learn 10 hooks** for 0–9 (0=ball, 1=pencil, 2=swan, 3=heart, 4=sailboat, 5=snake, 6=lollipop, 7=flag, 8=snowman, 9=balloon). One week of practice: digit → image instantly.

**TOP 10 list.** Memorize 10 largest cities, 10 presidents, 10 something. Image of the rank's hook + the item, vivid.

### Major system

**Master the digit→consonant table.** One week of Anki flashcards: digit ↔ consonant, both directions, automatic.

**Build your personal 00–20 image library.** For each, an image whose consonants match Major (e.g., 14 = "tour" or "tar"). Personalize — your images are stronger.

**Phone number.** Take a number (e.g., 502 138 749). Split into pairs: 50–21–38–74–9. Each pair → image. Build a story chain. After two mental run-throughs, you know it durably.

### Spaced repetition

**Your first Anki deck.** Download Anki (anki.app, free). Create the deck "Daily learning". Add 20 cards of something you want to remember. Open Anki **every day**. The algorithm shows what is due. Add 20 more weekly.

**Anki for understood material.** After reading a chapter, make 5–10 cards of key concepts — not dictionary definitions, but **thought-provoking** questions ("Why does the greenhouse effect strengthen with CO₂?", "What do mitochondria share with bacteria?"). Comprehension cards beat memorization cards.

### Active recall

**Close and write.** After a chapter — close the book, take a blank page, write everything you remember. Check what's missing. Repeat next day; usually you remember more, not less (testing effect).

**Explain to a child.** Pick a concept, explain aloud in plain language as if to a 10-year-old. Where you stumble — gap. Go back to source.

### Special challenges

**Card deck.** After 4 weeks of training, memorize a shuffled 52-card deck. Requires PAO + palace on 17 loci (3 cards per locus). Master time: <17 s. First attempt: 30 min — normal. After 2 months daily: 5 min.

**π digits.** Successive milestones: 10 (5 min for anyone), 50 (one evening with Major + palace), 100 (one week training), 500 (a month), 1,000 (six months). Records: Akira Haraguchi 100,000 (2006); Suresh Kumar Sharma 70,030 (Guinness, 2015).

**20 names at a party.** Use Face-Feature-Link: eye contact, repeat the name in conversation, find the most distinctive feature, convert name into an image, link in your mind. After 5–10 events: reflex.

**100 foreign words in a week.** Linkword + Anki: day 1 — 20 new words (each Linkword: sound key + image, then to Anki). Day 2 — Anki review + 15 new. Continue. Total in a week: 100. In a month: 400. In a year: ~5,000 (advanced B2).

### A 4-week beginner plan

- **Week 1.** Daily: 5 min warm-ups + 10 min building first palace + first shopping list.
- **Week 2.** Daily: 5 min warm-ups + 10 min Number-Shape + start Anki with 10 cards.
- **Week 3.** Daily: 5 min warm-ups + 10 min Major 0–9 + 10 min Anki.
- **Week 4.** Daily: 10 min Major 00–20 + 10 min Anki + try a phone number with Major.

After a month — the **foundation** on which you build for the rest of your life.

## B. Digital tools

### Spaced repetition

- **Anki** (anki.app) — open source, the canon. Algorithms: SM-2 + FSRS. Huge community, thousands of ready decks (medicine, languages, law).
- **SuperMemo** (super-memo.com) — Polish original by Piotr Woźniak. Most refined algorithm (SM-18). Unique **incremental reading**.
- **RemNote** (remnote.com) — combines notes, mind maps, and SR.
- **Quizlet** (quizlet.com) — popular, lighter, good for beginners and children.
- **Memrise** (memrise.com) — language-focused, community-built mnemonic memes.
- **Mochi** (mochi.cards) — minimalist, Markdown-first.

### Notes and knowledge graphs

- **Obsidian** (obsidian.md) — local Markdown notes connected as a knowledge graph; SR plugins.
- **Notion** — notes + databases + tasks.
- **Logseq** — open-source local graph.
- **XMind / MindNode / Coggle** — classical mind maps.
- **Roam Research** — bidirectional linking pioneer.

### Brain training

Critical view: most apps give local effects (better at the apps), weak transfer to real life. Better: Anki + real skills.

Exception: for seniors, brain-training apps have small benefits in maintaining function.

- **BrainHQ** (brainhq.com) — strongest research base (Posit Science, 300+ publications).
- **Lumosity** (lumosity.com) — most popular, controversial (FTC $50M settlement 2016).
- **Peak / Elevate / CogniFit** — alternatives.

### Memory sport

- **Memory League** (memoryleague.com) — international training and competition platform.
- **Art of Memory** (artofmemory.com) — encyclopaedia, forum, tutorials.
- **Magnetic Memory Method** (magneticmemorymethod.com) — Anthony Metivier's blog and courses.

### Languages

- **Duolingo** — gamified start; combine with Anki for serious.
- **Anki + own cards** — unbeatable for serious language work.
- **Pimsleur** — audio conversational classic.
- **Glossika** — sentence-based SR.
- **Tandem / HelloTalk** — language exchange; irreplaceable.

### Focus

- **Forest** — Pomodoro + plant a virtual tree.
- **Freedom / Cold Turkey** — block distractions on schedule.
- **Brain.fm / Endel** — music designed for focus.

### Sleep and meditation

- **Sleep Cycle / AutoSleep** — sleep tracking.
- **Yoga Nidra** (Insight Timer, YouTube) — conscious rest.
- **Calm / Headspace / Insight Timer** — meditation.

### Choose minimum

Start: **Anki + a notes app + a language app** (if learning a language). Add after 3 months: meditation, Pomodoro. For enthusiasts: SuperMemo, Memory League, RemNote.

What you don't need: expensive brain training (unless you are a senior with specific evidence), ten apps at once. The best tool is the one **you use daily**.

## C. A personal path — how to start and not give up

### Diagnosis

Before starting, answer honestly: 1. Why are you doing this? 2. How much daily time do you have? 3. What have you tried; what worked / didn't? 4. Your biggest weakness? 5. Your age? 6. Are you sleeping, moving, eating well?

### Pick one foundational technique

Most common beginner mistake: trying everything at once → chaos → giving up. Better: one technique, master it, then add another after 3–4 weeks.

Recommendations by need:
- **Exam in 6 months** → Anki + Active Recall.
- **Foreign language** → Linkword + Anki.
- **Public speaking** → Memory palace.
- **Names** → Face-Feature-Link.
- **Numbers / dates** → Major system.
- **General fitness, curiosity** → Memory palace.
- **Dementia prevention** → Aerobic + new skill (instrument).

### First 30 days

- **Week 1 — foundation.** Daily 10 min. Set fixed time and place. Pick one technique. Do exercises from section A. Keep a simple log.
- **Week 2 — first real test.** Use the technique in a real situation (shopping list, day plan, words, names). Check the result. Celebrate (the brain loves rewards).
- **Week 3 — expand.** Increase scale (10 → 20 items, palace on 10 → 20 loci). Add a complementary second technique.
- **Week 4 — review and decide.** Look at the log. What works / doesn't? Plan next month with corrections. If no progress at all in a month of honest practice — likely the technique doesn't fit your goal. Switch.

### The 3-month plan

- **Month 2:** master the chosen technique. Mastery in palace on 30 loci, fluency in Major to 99, first 1,000 words of a language.
- **Month 3:** integration. Combine techniques into your **personal system**.

After 90 days you feel a **qualitative difference**. Not "I know three techniques" — you **think differently**. You look at new information and automatically ask: "how do I encode this so it stays?"

### The yearly plan

- **Months 4–6:** depth and specialization.
- **Months 7–9:** transfer — apply in everyday life.
- **Months 10–12:** teach others. (Teaching is the strongest retention.)

### Habits that sustain practice

1. Fixed time and place.
2. Streak — but no fanaticism. If you break, start again.
3. Practice journal — even one sentence a day.
4. Habit stacking — attach to an existing habit ("right after morning coffee").
5. Environment — Anki on the home screen, notebook on the desk, mental palaces during boring meetings.

### Common reasons people quit

- "No time" — really: no priority. You have 30 min for social media, you have 15 for learning. Choice, not time.
- "Too hard" — first palace is clumsy. Normal. The brain learns through error.
- "No effects" — look at the data. Anki shows statistics. Without data — easy to quit before effects show.
- "Going too far too fast" — three-hour daily plans → burnout in a week. Start with **less than feels bearable**. 5 min. Increase only after a month.
- "Life happens" — illness, holidays, crisis. Natural. Return the next day, not two months later. The habit lives by repetition, not perfection.

### After a year

- New words / dates / names — multiple times faster.
- Texts read more deeply — because you actively work with them.
- Less reliance on notes.
- Less stress before talks / exams — you have tools.
- A different attitude to learning.
- More confidence — memory is a foundation of competence.

After two years: a **different cognitive life**. What seems impossible today (recite a card deck, 30-minute talk without notes, knowledge of 10,000 foreign words) becomes normal.

### A closing word

The art of memory is one of the most beautiful gifts culture has left us. Simonides did not have Anki — but taught humanity memory for 2,500 years. Cicero had no smartphone — yet remembered hours of speech. Aquinas wrote the *Summa* from his head. Camillo designed a theatre meant to hold the entire universe.

You do not use these techniques not because they are hard. You do not use them because no one told you about them.

Now you know.

**The next step is yours.**
