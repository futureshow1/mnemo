# 14. The Golden Dawn and Aleister Crowley — the art of memory in the 19th–20th-century hermetic tradition

> *„Memoria est rerum thesaurus." — Cicero, cited by Crowley in the heading of Liber Thisharb*

---

## I. The line of continuity from Bruno to London 1888

After Bruno's trial (1600) and the triumph of Cartesian rationalism, hermetic mnemonics **goes underground**. For nearly three centuries it is cultivated in Masonic lodges, Rosicrucian circles, private occult libraries. It returns only in the second half of the 19th century, when Britain is swept by an occult revival: Eliphas Lévi in France codifies the theory of magic, Helena Blavatsky founds the Theosophical Society (1875), and an esoteric wing rises within Freemasonry.

In 1888, three Masons — **William Wynn Westcott** (London coroner), **Samuel Liddell MacGregor Mathers** (self-taught genius, Kabbalah scholar) and **William Robert Woodman** (medical doctor) — found in London the **Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn**. The inspiration are the mysterious "Cipher Manuscripts" — allegedly received from Germany, in fact most likely a compilation by Westcott himself.

### Why the Golden Dawn is Bruno's direct heir

| Bruno (16th c.) | Golden Dawn (19th–20th c.) |
|---|---|
| *Corpus Hermeticum*, Christian Kabbalah, magia naturalis | The same sources + tarot, yoga, alchemy, Dee's Enochian |
| Mind properly ordered = microcosm of cosmos | "Magus = perfect microcosm" — Mathers |
| Archetypal images on memory wheels | Correspondence tables + Kabbalistic Tree of Life |
| 30 statues of Lampas triginta statuarum | 10 sephiroth × multiple columns = hundreds of images |
| Latin mnemonic works | Knowledge Lectures + Mathers Table |
| Burned by the Inquisition | Secret, oath of silence, hierarchy of grades |

Initiation in the Golden Dawn **required memorising** an enormous table of correspondences — Hebrew alphabet, sephiroth, planets, zodiac signs, colours, sounds, tarot cards, angelic names, and spirit hierarchies. Without it, the next grade was closed. **This was a mnemonic undertaking on a Brunonian scale — in 19th-century London.**

## II. Knowledge Lectures — five grades of mnemonic initiation

Mathers created **five Knowledge Lectures**, one for each grade of the Outer Order. Every adept passed an **oral examination before initiation** — reciting dozens of tables, alphabets and correspondences from memory.

| Grade | Hebrew | Sephira | What to memorise |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 = 0 Neophyte** | — | — | Hebrew alphabet (22 letters + gematria), 10 sephira names, 3 pillars of the Tree, 4 worlds of Kabbalah, basic planetary and elemental signs |
| **1 = 10 Zelator** | מלכות | Malkuth | Divine Name ADNI, Archangel Sandalphon, Choir of Angels Ashim, 4 elements with Arabic nomenclature, signs of Earth, grade-1 geomancy |
| **2 = 9 Theoricus** | יסוד | Yesod | Full Yesod table + Moon symbolism, Air signs, **Enochian alphabet** of John Dee (24 letters), 12 zodiac signs |
| **3 = 8 Practicus** | הוד | Hod | Hod table, Mercury, **16 geomantic figures** with attributions, Water signs, Aristotle-Lull tree of life |
| **4 = 7 Philosophus** | נצח | Netzach | Netzach table, Venus, Fire signs, path to the Portal, Kabbalistic gematria, foundations of Enochian |

The **Mathers Table** was the core instrument — a vast cross-index of **32 paths + 10 sephiroth × multiple columns** (names, colours, myths, perfumes, plants, metals, demons, angels, tarot cards, spirit hierarchies).

### 777 — operational database of the whole system

What Mathers had kept secret in the Golden Dawn manuscripts, **Crowley published in 1909 as *777 vel Prolegomena Symbolica*** — breaking his oath of silence and causing a schism with Mathers. *777* is not a ritual Liber — it is the **structural foundation for all the others**, the operational database of the entire hermetic system.

**What 777 contains exactly:**

- **32 paths of the Tree of Life** (10 sephiroth + 22 connections) crossed with **191 columns of correspondences** (Crowley's own count)
- **Hebrew and Greek** Divine Names, archangels, angelic choirs, Goetic demon hierarchies
- **colours in 4 scales** of the Golden Dawn: King (male sephiroth), Queen (female sephiroth), Prince (male paths), Princess (female paths)
- perfumes, precious stones, plants, animals, tastes, sounds
- **Greco-Roman, Egyptian, Hindu, Gnostic, Scandinavian** myths — each column as a separate tradition
- **alphabets**: Hebrew, Greek, Arabic, **Enochian** (John Dee's)
- **geomancy, astrology, tarot, ceremonial magic** in a single cross-grid
- **Tarot card attributions** to the paths of the Tree of Life (the template still used in the Rider-Waite-Smith deck)

**Crowley called 777 „the magickal memory of mankind".** His introduction is one of his best texts on hermetic method: it explains **what mnemonic analogy is as a cognitive tool**, why the correspondences are not arbitrary, how to build your own. A **mnemonic manifesto**.

**Practical mechanism of 777:** if you know ONE column for a given sephira, through 777 you find all the others. Want to memorize Yesod (9th sephira)? *777* says: planet Moon, colour pearl-silver, stone quartz and pearl, perfume jasmine, deity Diana / Artemis / Chandra, archangel Gabriel, choir Cherubim, animal elephant, myth Zeus becoming a swan. **Each column is a separate mnemonic hook for the same idea.** Remember one — you remember them all. This is an embodiment of the hypertext principle „every node connected to every other".

This is **the most demanding mnemonic ritual in Western occult history**. Modern comparison: mastering the full Mathers stack + *777* equals learning **8,000—10,000 Anki cards** in fully contextualised narrative and symbolic form.

## III. Three memory-visualisation techniques of the Golden Dawn

### 1. Tattwa Vision

Five symbol-cards of the elements from the Indian Vedanta tradition, adapted by Mathers to Western Kabbalah:

| Tattwa | Symbol | Colour | Element |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Akasha** | oval egg | black | aether |
| **Vayu** | disk | blue | air |
| **Tejas** | triangle | red | fire |
| **Apas** | crescent | silver | water |
| **Prithivi** | square | yellow | earth |

**Practical procedure:**

1. Lay the card on a white sheet in bright light.
2. Gaze without blinking for 3–5 minutes, focusing all attention on shape and colour.
3. A **flashing colour** appears — the complementary colour pulses around the card's edges. A red triangle starts to throb green.
4. Close your eyes. The image "flies off" to the astral — becoming a window into inner vision.
5. "Step through" the window mentally: a landscape, figure, archetype appears.

Crowley said: **the best visualisation training he ever encountered.**

**Tattwa combinatorics:** tattwas can be layered. Egg (aether) in square (earth) = inner aether of the Earth element. Gives **25 base combinations** (5 × 5), further extensions to 125 (3 elements) and beyond. A **direct analogy to Bruno's wheels** — a combinatorial generator of unique mental addresses.

### 2. Pathworking

A mental journey along one of the **22 paths** linking the 10 sephiroth of the Tree of Life. Each path corresponds to a **Hebrew letter + Major Arcanum tarot card + astrological route**.

**Procedure:**

1. Choose a path — say Pe (פ), the Tower, between Hod (Mercury) and Netzach (Venus).
2. Sit comfortably, eyes closed, deep breath for 3 minutes.
3. Visualise the Tower tarot card in full detail (flame, falling figures, lightning).
4. The card "expands" — becoming a doorway. Step through.
5. Walk the path. Meet archetypes. Undergo initiation.
6. Return by the same route. Record in your Magical Record.

**What this actually trains:**
- Spatial imagination to a hypertrophic level
- Multi-layered associative memory (letter + sephira A + sephira B + card + archangel + animal + perfume + colour + myth + planet)
- The ability to hold **32 structural elements simultaneously**
- Active imagination (Jung's term, the technique older)

A full pathworking of all 22 paths is a mnemonic expedition through the entire symbolic cosmos — a full year's work for the adept.

### 3. Scrying in the Spirit Vision

A method of insight into a symbolic image — crystal ball, obsidian mirror, deck of cards, but mainly an **internal Tattwic image**. The adept builds a space in imagination, "enters" it, explores it, returns.

A direct heir of Bruno's *theatrum memoriae*: **memory as active space** through which one moves, not a static image on a plate. Contemporary research into mental imagery (Stephen Kosslyn, Harvard) confirms: this type of imagination training measurably enriches the functional structure of the visual cortex on fMRI.

## IV. Aleister Crowley — radicalisation of the system

**Edward Alexander "Aleister" Crowley** (1875–1947) joined the Golden Dawn in 1898. Just six years later, after his experience in Cairo in 1904 (the reception of *Liber AL vel Legis*), he creates his own system **Thelema** and the organisation **A∴A∴** (Astrum Argentum, "Silver Star"). Crowley **systematises memory-visualisation techniques**, combining:

- the strict discipline of the Golden Dawn
- Indian yoga (stay in India 1901–1905, masters of dharana)
- modern scientific method (timed experiments, quantitative journaling)
- his own experimental mnemonics

Four canonical A∴A∴ texts are crucial for the mnemonist.

### 1. Liber E vel Exercitiorum (1909) — Liber 9

The basic A∴A∴ training manual. Section V describes **dharana** — first concentration:

> Constrain the mind to concentrate itself upon a single simple object imagined. The five tatwas are useful for this purpose; they are: a black oval; a blue disk; a silver crescent; a yellow square; a red triangle.

**Crowley's progression:**

| Stage | Object | Time target |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | one simple object (one tattwa) | 1 minute uninterrupted |
| 2 | two simple objects (egg in square) | 3 minutes |
| 3 | complex stationary object (deck of cards, candle) | 5 minutes |
| 4 | complex moving object (pendulum, wheel) | 10 minutes |
| 5 | luminous object (flame, star) | 30 minutes |
| 6 | living object (animal, human figure) | one hour |

Iron rule: **keep a journal of exact holding times**. The adept's initial average = a few seconds. Goal = an hour uninterrupted.

Crowley writes brutally (*Magick Book 4*, Part I):

> The physical agony of achieving asana is trivial compared with the horrible tedium of Dharana, which may seem amusing for the first week but apparently gets worse as the practice teaches you what you are doing. Thoughts return "again and again to the charge" and "become a positive nightmare."

Modern mindfulness apps never disclose this brutal truth. Crowley was more honest — and more effective.

### 2. Liber O vel Manus et Sagittae (1909) — Liber 6

Practical instruction in the use of imagination for magical operations. The most important part: the **Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram (LBRP)** — a daily ritual of clearing mental space that **requires holding simultaneously in imagination:**

- **four archangels** around the practitioner (Raphael in the east, Gabriel in the west, Michael in the south, Uriel in the north)
- **four Kabbalistic Divine Names** in their proper colours (Yod-He-Vau-He, Adonai, Eheieh, Agla)
- **pentagrams** drawn with the blade of the hand in air in a precise geometric sequence
- **Hebrew letters** blazing with flame
- the **cosmic axis** of the Tree of Life stretched through the adept's body

The full ritual lasts 5–10 minutes and **must be from memory**. Daily practice for 6 months = the mnemonic analogue of an annual Anki session, but in a single instance of **synchronised multi-channel visualisation** (inner sight + bodily motion + voice + emotion).

### 3. Liber Resh vel Helios (1911) — Liber 200

**Four daily solar adorations** — the most practical A∴A∴ exercise:

| Time | Solar aspect | Egyptian deity | Sign |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dawn** | Ra-Hoor-Khuit | falcon-headed Ra | Sign of the Rising |
| **Noon** | Ahathoor | cow-headed Hathor | Sign of the Opening Lotus |
| **Sunset** | Tum | god of primordial completeness | Sign of the Setting |
| **Midnight** | Khephra | scarab | Sign of the Hidden |

**What this actually trains:**

1. **Day's rhythm as mnemonic** — four anchor moments to which the adept returns daily regardless of place, work, travel.
2. **Multi-sensory engagement** — image + movement + word + emotion simultaneously.
3. **Embodied memory** — fourfold daily repetition for a year ≈ 1,500 reps; the technique enters body, not intellect.
4. **Awareness of the cosmic cycle** — the adept stops losing track of time, because four times a day one is "synchronised".

The **legacy of Liber Resh** strikingly anticipates modern chronobiology (Andrew Huberman, Matthew Walker): the four adorations are a practical schedule of light and circadian ritual, exactly what neurobiologists now recommend for healthy sleep rhythm and mood.

### 4. Liber ThIShARB Viae Memoriae sub figura CMXIII (1913)

**Crowley's most important mnemonic text.** The Hebrew title ThIShARB (תישארב) is the reverse reading of BRShIT (בראשית) — the first word of the Torah ("Bereshit", "In the beginning"). **The key is in the title: we remember the world backwards.**

#### Main technique — Reverse Meditation

> Think backwards. Think the act of coming into this room. Think the act of walking towards the room. And so going on, thinking backwards on all the acts that you have done that day, coming to yourself waking up in the morning, perhaps remembering a few dreams, and then remembering your last thoughts as you went to sleep the night before. — *Liber ThIShARB, §9*

A brutally hard exercise. The brain is neurally configured for **forward narrative** — the arrow of time, cause-effect. Going backwards **breaks that automatism** and opens new access to episodic memory.

#### 7-day starter plan for Liber Thisharb (reconstructed for beginners)

| Day | Range | Session length | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | last **5 minutes** | 4× daily | Just learn to think backwards. Hard. |
| **2** | last **15 minutes** | 3× daily | Small details, gestures, who said what. |
| **3** | last **hour** | 2× daily | A meal backwards, the chair backwards, the road backwards. |
| **4** | **half a day** | 1× in the evening | From dinner to breakfast, step by step. |
| **5** | **whole day** | 1× before sleep | From "I lie down" to "I woke up this morning". |
| **6** | **yesterday** + today | 2× | Verify that traces are stable |
| **7** | **a week backwards** | 1× | Day by day, from today to last Saturday |

Crowley suggests **continuation after the first two months**: previous month, previous year, childhood, birth. Advanced stage (officially): before birth — alleged "past life."

**Crowley's official aim:** insight into past lives (he was a Western Buddhist).
**Scientific goal:** training episodic memory to a level inaccessible to a normal person.
**Therapeutic goal** (not named by Crowley but today called EMDR and exposure therapy): revising one's own day breaks down automatic emotional reactions, gives access to unnoticed details (microexpressions, overheard phrases, fragments of image).

#### Alternative exercises in Liber Thisharb

- **§10:** Recite the alphabet backwards. Days of the week from Saturday to Sunday. Months from December to January. Numbers 100 to 1 in a fast chain like a prayer.
- **§11:** Read a book whose pages are reordered backwards. Three pages a day.
- **§12:** Recite a poem you know well — backwards, word by word. Classics: any well-known poem from the last line up.

**What this gives the mnemonist:**

- Episodic memory **freed from narrative automatism**
- Flexibility in searching memories (not only "from the beginning")
- Awareness of the *constructive* nature of memory (every recall is an act, not a reproduction — a point Bartlett would confirm scientifically in 1932)
- A new level of working memory — holding thousands of small details of the day in the buffer

### 5. Magical Record — diary as mnemonic

Crowley required every adept to keep a **daily Magical Record** — a diary describing **every** magical operation, **every** meditation, **every** dream, **every** meal (yes — literally), **every** weather, **every** emotion, **every** encounter.

The diary becomes:

- **external memory** — the analogue of modern Obsidian / Roam Research / Notion
- **mirror** for reverse meditation — re-reading a year-old entry reactivates the full context
- **database** for studying one's own patterns (Crowley pioneered self-tracking — 100 years before the Quantified Self movement)
- **Mathers Table for the self**

Crowley published his own *The Magical Record of the Beast 666* (1914–1920) — one of the most detailed personal documents of the 20th century. A modern reader finds there the exact temperature of the room, the quality of coffee, the intensity of migraine, the content of dreams, recorded daily for six years.

### 6. Liber Astarte vel Berylli (1916) — Liber 175

A mnemonic of **bhakti yoga** — concentration on a chosen deity. The adept selects **one deity** (Isis, Shiva, Jesus, Krishna), builds an absolutely precise image (colour of robes, attributes, facial expression, surroundings), carries that image in mind **through all moments of the day** for a week, a month, a year. Until the image "comes alive" — starts to respond, to speak, to act.

This is the **Brunonian archetypal figure** carried into the 20th century. Goal: a durability of the mnemonic image otherwise unattainable. Regardless of spiritual interpretation, the technique lays the groundwork for modern *dual coding* (Paivio) on a yearly scale.

## V. Dion Fortune and the Society of the Inner Light

**Violet Mary Firth "Dion Fortune"** (1890–1946) joined the Golden Dawn in 1919; in 1924 she founded the **Fraternity (later Society) of the Inner Light** — her own organisation that continued the Golden Dawn tradition in a **therapeutic and ethical** key. Her books:

- ***The Mystical Qabalah*** (1935) — the best introduction to hermetic Kabbalah; still read today.
- ***Psychic Self-Defence*** (1930) — the psychology of defence against mental influences.
- ***The Training and Work of an Initiate*** (1930) — systematic instruction for the adept.

### Fortune's contribution to mnemonics

Fortune **translates the Golden Dawn into the language of Jungian psychology**. After her books, Kabbalah ceases to be occult and becomes a **map of the psyche**. Sephiroth are not only cosmic entities but **psychic types**: Chesed = generosity, Geburah = severity, Tiphareth = central balance.

**What this means for the mnemonist:** every element of the Mathers Table gains a **second layer of meaning** — not only symbolic but psychological. One sinks deeper into the image, because the image corresponds to an inner part of the psyche. Mnemonics becomes self-therapeutic.

Fortune was also the first in the Golden Dawn tradition to **honestly speak of the dangers** of visualisation techniques — and to give concrete safety procedures (daily LBRP, grounding after pathworking, brief closing prayer).

## VI. Austin Osman Spare — the mnemonics of sigilisation

**Austin Osman Spare** (1886–1956), London visual artist, was briefly Crowley's pupil. He rejected the hierarchical mnemonics of the Golden Dawn and created a system of **personal sigilisation** — the most radical innovation in the history of Western magic since the 16th century.

### Spare's sigil method

1. **Formulate the intention** in a single declarative sentence, in the present tense: "I am currently writing the novel with ease". (Crowley would stop here. Spare transforms further.)
2. **Strike through all repeated letters**.
3. **Fuse the remaining letters into a single monogram** — a graphic mark illegible to the conscious mind.
4. **Gaze at the sigil** during "gnosis" (a brief state of self-loss — laughter, dance, scream, orgasm).
5. **Forget the sigil** — Spare claimed that the forgetting itself dispatches the intention into the subconscious, where it works **without ego interference**.

### What this has to do with mnemonics

Spare discovered **the other side of mnemonics**: instead of remembering consciously — **depositing in the subconscious** via a condensed image-mark. This is the **mnemonic underground**: the mark is to function not as a label for retrieval, but as an anchor of hidden intent.

Modern applications: marketing sigil-makers (logos, brand marks — Apple, Nike, Adidas are *de facto* modern sigils), graphic facilitation, **mnemonics for artists** (not about recall, but about embodiment). Chaos magic of the 20th and 21st century (Peter Carroll, Phil Hine) directly cites Spare.

## VII. What of this tradition is current for today's mnemonist

A translation into non-esoteric language. Every point below can be used without belief in magic.

### 1. Reverse reconstruction of the day (Liber Thisharb)

The **strongest** episodic-memory exercise known to the West. **10 minutes before sleep**, 7 days a week. After a month you'll notice you remember details of conversations you never consciously remembered. After half a year — you'll have access to days from years ago with a precision you didn't have even the day after. Without a word of past lives.

### 2. Daily journal (Magical Record)

In modern translation: a short bullet journal with entries on body state, emotions, material read, dream images. The Magical Record without magic. **Ten minutes in the evening across a year = 36 hours of detailed self-insight.**

### 3. Pathworking as a technique for students

Pick a physical place — say, the path between two rooms — as a **mnemonic "route"**. Assign each thematic category of material to one station. This is literally the **Brunonian palace enriched with astrological correspondences** — only instead of astrology you use your domain's terminology.

### 4. Tattwa Vision as visualisation training

A doorway to the memory palace. **5 cards, 5 minutes a day for a week** yields a greater ability to generate vivid images than any other known training. Try it — it's a concrete, measurable effect.

### 5. The fourfold daily rhythm (Liber Resh)

Without religious content — as **anchor cognitive prevention**: dawn, noon, sunset, midnight as **four moments of "awakened attention"**. Today you'd call it *time-anchoring* or *circadian mindfulness*. 30 seconds × 4 = 2 minutes a day that restructure the day.

### 6. The Mathers correspondence stack

The analogue of modern **concept mapping**. Medical students do it for body systems, master mnemonists — for digits and decks, Mathers — for the whole symbolic cosmos. Scale gives the result: once one correspondence table is mastered, every further fitting element is remembered **effortlessly**.

### 7. Sigilisation as subconscious mnemonics (Spare)

For artists, designers, makers: instead of remembering consciously, **deposit intent in a geometric mark** and forget. Modern applications: brand logo, design-intent sigil, sigil-seal of a book.

## VIII. Critique and warning

- Crowley wrote in **barbaric 19th-century esoteric language** that for a modern reader may be off-putting or confusing. The techniques themselves are concrete and work.
- Some Golden Dawn adepts **lost mental health** to intensive visualisation work. **Liber Thisharb's reverse meditation done without breaks** can trigger dissociative episodes. It is not a game — Fortune's *Psychic Self-Defence* was no accident.
- **The past-life claims** have no scientific support. What does — a powerful episodic-memory technique, **regardless** of how one interprets its source.
- Crowley was a deeply problematic figure ethically (authoritarianism, drug abuse, lies to adepts, mistreatment of women). The fact that his techniques work **does not make his life a model**. Things are otherwise with Mathers, Westcott and especially Dion Fortune — proper Victorian intellectuals leading exemplary lives.
- **Spare** died destitute in a London slum. His sigilisation worked artistically, not economically.
